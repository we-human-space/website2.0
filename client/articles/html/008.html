Pursuing Radiance

Sacha – The Mystery of Radiance

You might be more acquainted with radiance than you think. The English languages is full of references to it:

“It’s your time to shine”
“She’s glowing”
“He’s beaming with pride”
“She exudes/radiates confidence”
“He’s brilliant”
“She’s a star”
“He lights up the room”

Depictions of yogis, prophets & saints show them surrounded by light. Steve Jobs had such a noticeable presence that his employees called it a reality-distortion-field. We say of great leaders that they had an aura of power.

What’s going on here? Could it be that just as we perceive up light & sound from a person, we can perceive emanations from their inner nature? I think so. It doesn’t seem like a stretch to suppose we’re energetically attuned to the inner states of those who surround us. After all, we’re hard-wired social creatures.

Now, is this just body language we’re picking up? Are we merely creating visual & energetic metaphors to explain macro/micro level body language cues from people? Or is this a real emanation of energy we’re picking up — like tuning in to an energetic field? We’ll leave that question for another time.

The Darkside Of Forced Radiance

One of the biggest issues facing us today is that we take outward signs to be more important than the inner substance. We take the footage to be more important than the experience itself.

Social media newsfeeds, similarly, are chalk full poorly veiled self-aggrandizement. Any beautiful moment now seems embedded with the imperative to document & share.

Slowly, the value of the event starts to depend on its appraisal in the eyes of the witnesses. We attach our self-worth to our outward appearance. In response, people have gotten extremely good at simulating outward signs, at the expense of the real shit. “Looking like” becomes more important than being. Believe me, I know. For years I lost sight of myself. Like so many others, I became fixated on the simulacrum to the point of losing the original.

This is not radiance. This is forced radiance. Simulated radiance. Which is truly a tragedy, because it misses the point or radiance entirely.

You see the point of radiance… is the experience of radiance. It requires no witness. Radiance is its own reward.

So why do we fake radiance? Why do we want to be witnessed? To be praised as stars? Why the perverse attachment — not to the inner experience of shining — but to outward appearance?

Because, deep down, we think that without footage, it’s fiction…

Every like says “you are loved”.
Every mention says “you exist”.
Every share says “you belong”.

How has that been working out for you? It’s been pretty shitty for me. Every time we enjoy one of these shallow dopamine bursts, we invest ourselves more deeply into matters of surface.

Fuck. That.

True Radiance

I can tell you very little on radiance. I’m just learning to experience its truth myself. What I can tell you from the progress I’ve made so far, is that it’s all about letting go. Letting go of the images which dictate how you should be. Letting down the walls which defend your ego. Divesting from the approval of others. Following your inner truth for its own sake.

Simply being.

Doing without forcing. Without expectations on the outcome. Let your inner voice guide you, and you’ll become one with it.

I leave you with this 24th verse of eternal wisdom from the Tao Te Ching — supreme teachings which provided the bedrock for Buddhism:

He who stands on tiptoe
doesn't stand firm.
He who rushes ahead
doesn't go far.
He who tries to shine
dims his own light.
He who defines himself
can't know who he really is.
He who has power over others
can't empower himself.
He who clings to his work
will create nothing that endures.

If you want to accord with the Tao,
just do your job, then let go.

— Shine on you crazy diamonds. Shine on.

Sacha's Book Recommendation

Book: Tao Te Ching
Author: Lao Tzu
Translator: Stephen Mitchell

Upon my first listen to this book, in audio format, I fell into a profound bliss. I experienced the most absolute feeling of inner peace in my short life thus far. The teachings have a serene truth to them. With mystical tenderness, it seems to gently draw out the darkness from your soul and fill you with truth.

If haven’t yet encountered the The Book Of The Way, then it is my honour to bestow this gift upon you. To live in harmony with these 81 verses is to experience true radiance.

Though many of the verses remain a mystery to me, I sense that I’ll awaken to their truth once I’m ready. Of those that I understand, not one is uncherished.
