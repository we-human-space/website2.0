Pursuing the Smiles of Life

Gregory - After two years of experience with startups, I’ve learned that almost nothing comes easily. The key is to take 100% responsibility for your actions and get to work. As a founder, everything is hinging on you. The mental strength required to push through is immense, but the rewards are huge.

That’s why I resonate so much with Bruce Lee when he says: “Do not wish for an easy life. Wish for the strength to endure a difficult one”.

Dreams are hard to accomplish. They take focus & continuous effort. I believe that as a part of this generation — a generation more equipped and enlightened than ever, everyone has the duty to stand up and take action for their dreams. If you don't stand up for yourself, who will?

Stay strong seekers

Sacha - The Smile of Life is a passing event. You can’t expect to be grinning every minute you spend working on your life’s task. Currently, I’m studying electronic music & sound design in Sydney. It’s amazing. I’m on my path to mastering the craft of music, which makes me tremendously happy. But it doesn’t always feel like that. Sometimes I spend hours trying to coax out a song on Ableton Live, only to end up with a steaming pile of shit. Sometimes I don’t even bother pressing Save. But I don’t give up. Why?

Because I’ve had a Smile of Life. Or five. Or forty. I forget. But it doesn’t matter. Because now when I experience a brief moment where everything is right in the world, I use it to cancel out every single shitty hour spent struggling to get it tight. It overrides everything else. That’s a decision.

To follow your path, you have to make an effigy out of your Smiles of Life. Your moments of completeness & deep fulfillment, need to be blown out of proportion and become your North Star. You need to develop a religious devotion to these moments, understanding that you must suffer and sacrifice so that you may receive them occasionally. This is the only path to mastery.

Sacha - I've stumbled upon a crucial resource for musicians, music aficionados & audiophiles. The modern world is so polluted with sound that many of us have forgotten how to listen. To hear with a Beginner’s Mind — like a Buddhist master or a world-class composer — we must clean out our ears. More importantly, we must clean out our minds. We have a notion that some sounds are background noise, a nuisance or a distraction, and that others are important & valuable. This skews our perception, taking us further away from reality, and from a master’s understanding of music.

The author Schaefer takes us inside sound itself, so we might feel it viscerally, in our gut. He introduces us to its intrinsic imagery — light & dark, heaven & hell, texture & line — so we might develop a kind of synaesthesia; seeing music in our minds' eye.

That being said, learn to hear once again and go deeper than ever before into one of your dominant senses with that short pdf read.


Chloé's Book Recommendation

Man's Search for Meaning is a great book to keep in your library and reread a few times, especially when you feel lost in your life; when you cannot find any source of Smiles of Life and when you feel overwhelmed or discouraged by negative events or thoughts. The author reminds us what our souls are truly looking for: meaning. Not distraction, neither pleasure.

Book: Man's Search for Meaning

Author: Viktor E. Frankl

To put you into context, Frankl, a psychiatrist, talks about his own survival in Nazi death camps. How did he managed to pursue life while being separated from his wife and children, living in horrible conditions and seeing his friends giving up on life and dying?

The key answer and takeaway: he found meaning in his suffering by choosing love over fear. Indeed, Frankl shows how love always wins over fear on the long term, no matter what situation you'll find yourself into.
